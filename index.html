About

Hi, I'm Ananth Rao. I'm a senior at Rutgers University triple majoring in Mechanical Engineering, Computer Science, and Applied Physics with a minor in Mathematics. I'm 21 years old, shorter than average (but not too much!), and Indian. Every day, I code, I build, I learn, I read, I study, I practice, I meet someone new, and I help someone. I go to a hackathon almost every weekend, and I teach music whenever I don't. I like to draw, play tennis, sing, play music, write stories, and have long philosophical discussions with a friend in the wee hours of the morning after we've both put in a hard night's work.

When I tell people about myself; my background, my studies, my habits in college, I usually get something along the line of, "Why would you do that to yourself?"

Honestly, I don't get it. How could college be more about what you don't do, than what you do? Isn't the purpose of college to learn as much as possible? Truth is, the only thing I have done to myself is to cheat myself out of opportunities to learn. Every moment spent not reading about something innovative, every moment not spent hacking or building something new: every moment not working on an amazing idea, every moment not spent learning more about people, places, and the world is a breath of life I could have better used. And that is something I strive to correct every day.

I am constantly both fascinated and terrified by the vast ocean of things I do not know. And the more I learn, the more my eyes open. I often find myself immersed in new worlds of ideas and information that I'd never even dreamed of. It's truly overwhelming. I often ask myself, "How can I stay afloat in a boundless sea? How can I learn? I'll never be able to learn everything I would like to, so why even try?"

I try because knowledge is enchanting. The Pierian Spring is my stimulant, and I am hopelessly addicted. I'll never rest while I still have something I don't understand. If it takes forever, I'm willing to stay the course. All I can do is keep inbibing that vast ocean, one sip at a time. All I can do is keep wondering, keep thinking. Keep building, keep learning. And who knows? Along the way, I might change the world.

My name is Ananth Rao. I am an aspiring designer, developer, physicist, entrepreneur, engineer, philosopher, teacher, musician, composer, actor, artist, writer, and lifelong learner. Boy, that was a mouthful. I only hope to be able to call myself even half of those someday. And with luck - and a lot of hard work, I will.

I'm always interested, always excited, to work on new projects and new ideas, or even help in any small way that I can, so feel free to contact me. My greatest goal in life is to help as many people as I can, to learn as much as I can, and to use any knowledge I may acquire to make the world a better place.

Truly yours,

Ananth Rao
